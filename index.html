Race sign - Logarithmic Function

Transformations

I began by identifying that the shape of my graph closely resembled a logarithmic function, so I selected the parent function \( y = \log(x) \) as a starting point. From observing the orientation of the curve in my image, I noticed that it was decreasing from left to right rather than increasing, which indicated a reflection across the y-axis. I therefore modified the function to \( y = \log(-x) \) to match the direction of the graph.

Initially, I found it difficult to determine the correct vertical stretch or compression factor because the graph in the image was positioned far from the origin, making it challenging to accurately compare steepness. To address this, I temporarily shifted the image so that the vertical asymptote aligned with \( x = 0 \). This allowed me to focus only on the shape of the graph without the added complexity of translations. By comparing the rate of change, I determined that a vertical compression was needed rather than a stretch, and refined the value through observation until the curve closely matched the image.

Once the shape was established, I returned the graph to its original position by applying translations. I shifted the graph horizontally to align the vertical asymptote with its position in the image and then applied a vertical translation so the curve passed through key points. This resulted in the function \[ y = 0.75 \log\bigl(-(x - 1.5)\bigr) + 2.75 \] which closely models the shape in the photograph.

Summary of Transformations:
• Reflection on the y-axis → \( \log(x) \rightarrow \log(-x) \)
• Vertical compression of factor 0.75 → \( 0.75 \log(-x) \)
• Horizontal translation of 1.5 units to the right → \( 0.75 \log\bigl(-(x - 1.5)\bigr) \)
• Vertical translation of 2.75 units upward → \( 0.75 \log\bigl(-(x - 1.5)\bigr) + 2.75 \)

Domain and Range

The domain of my function is \( D: \{x \in \mathbb{R} \mid 1.1 \le x \le 1.45\} \). I limited the domain to the portion of the sign that was visible in the photograph. In hindsight, I would have taken the picture so that it included the entire sign, allowing me to model the full curve rather than only the visible section. As a result, my model is only valid for the portion of the sign captured in the image.

Since the function is continuous, the range can be determined by evaluating the function at the endpoints of the domain. By calculating \( f(1.1) \) and \( f(1.45) \), I obtained approximate output values of 1.77 and 2.45, respectively.

Therefore, the range of the function is approximately \( R: \{f(x) \in \mathbb{R} \mid 1.77 \le f(x) \le 2.45\} \).
